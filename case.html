Juniper Networks Case Studies

Discover how Juniper Networks helped businesses transform their network infrastructure with innovative solutions.

1. Seagate: Transitioning to AI-Driven SD-WAN

Challenge: Seagate, a global leader in data storage solutions, needed a more scalable and cost-effective solution for their complex network infrastructure across 18 countries, moving away from traditional MPLS connections.

Solution: Juniper’s AI-Driven SD-WAN with Session Smart Routing optimized traffic routing. The solution offered intelligent routing, proactive network management, and enhanced security.

Outcome:

  • Cost Reduction by eliminating MPLS reliance.
  • Improved application performance through dynamic routing.
  • Better network visibility for proactive issue resolution.
  • Scalable solution to handle Seagate’s growing network needs.

2. First Bank: Modernizing Data Center Infrastructure

Challenge: First Bank needed to upgrade their data center to meet high-frequency transaction demands, ensure security, and comply with regulatory requirements.

Solution: Juniper implemented QFX5100 and EX4300 Switches, Apstra Automation, and SRX Series Firewalls to modernize their data center infrastructure.

Outcome:

  • Enhanced network resiliency for critical banking operations.
  • Improved security with SRX Series firewalls.
  • Faster service delivery through network automation.
  • Scalable infrastructure to accommodate future growth.

3. 5G Networks: Scaling Digital Services

Challenge: 5G Networks needed to scale its infrastructure to support high-speed, low-latency services for mobile and broadband users.

Solution: Juniper provided a high-performance solution with MX Series Routers, QFX and EX Series Switches, and SRX Series Firewalls to support 5G services.

Outcome:

  • Scalable infrastructure to meet growing 5G demands.
  • Reduced latency for real-time communication and data transfer.
  • Improved operational efficiency through automated management.
  • Future-proof network to support upcoming 5G advancements.
